# Topical Authority — jalan.co.id

## Role and boundary

Jalan.co.id should become an Indonesian reference for roads and streets across planning, ground/subgrade, drainage, pavement selection, geometry, pedestrian facilities, safety equipment, lighting, construction quality, maintenance, procurement, and handover. Readers include public/private asset owners, developers, consultants, contractors, facility/estate teams, communities, and students.

It covers road-system decisions and interfaces, not unsupported engineering design. Exact traffic, pavement, structural, drainage, electrical, accessibility, or traffic-control decisions require current Indonesian requirements, site data, and qualified professionals. Related owned domains may independently cover concrete, paving, lighting, steel, heavy equipment, safety, and contractors; cross-domain overlap is allowed. Cannibalization applies only among Jalan.co.id pages.

## Existing coverage and risks

- The apparent breadth is dominated by five cloned local-service families; these are doorway/crawl/cannibalization risks, not 470 distinct topics.
- Sitemap sources disagree by hundreds of URLs, creating indexing and audit ambiguity.
- Commercial hubs mix education, price, products, construction process, and sales intent.
- Road design, public safety, electrical lighting/signals, accessibility, and work-zone advice are high-stakes and require current primary sources and expert review.
- The portfolio boundary is broad: generic catalogs for concrete, lighting, poles, or safety products would dilute the road-system entity.

## Evidence and editorial standards

Use current Indonesian authority/standards sources and qualified road, geotechnical, drainage, transport, electrical, accessibility, and safety reviewers. Never invent pavement thickness, road width, slope, compaction, lighting, sign/marking, traffic, price, or service-life values. Case studies require real locations, permission, baseline, design basis, construction/QC evidence, dates, limitations, and outcomes.
